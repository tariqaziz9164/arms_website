About 30 Bore Pistols

Also known TT Pistol (Tula-Tokarev)

30 bore pistols are renowned for their precision and reliability. These pistols come in various models, each designed to meet specific needs, whether for personal defense, sport shooting, or professional use.

Caliber: 7.62x25mm Tokarev Bore: 7.62mm
Designer: Fedor Tokarev
Manufacturer: Various Soviet and Eastern Bloc arsenals
Introduced: 1930s
Action: Short-recoil, semi-automatic
Capacity: 8 rounds (detachable box magazine)

Notable Features

The TT pistol was designed by Fedor Tokarev and was first produced at the Tula Arsenal in the Soviet Union.
It was adopted by the Soviet military in the early 1930s as the standard issue sidearm, replacing the Nagant M1895 revolver.
The pistol is chambered in the powerful 7.62x25mm Tokarev cartridge, known for its high velocity and penetrating capabilities.
The TT features a simple and robust design, with a single-action trigger and an external hammer.
It became widely distributed and saw service not only in the Soviet Union but also in various Eastern Bloc countries and other nations during the Cold War.

Development and Service

The TT pistol was developed to meet the need for a modern, semi-automatic sidearm for the Soviet military. Its design was influenced by John Browning's FN Model 1903 pistol. The TT became a standard issue sidearm for the Red Army and Soviet police forces. During World War II, the TT pistol saw extensive use by Soviet forces. Its reliability and simplicity made it well-suited for the harsh conditions of the Eastern Front. The TT remained in service for several decades and was eventually replaced by the Makarov PM in the 1950s.

Russian-made 30 Bore Pistols

Russian-made 30 bore pistols have a storied history, reflecting the nation's commitment to producing reliable firearms for various purposes. The Russian firearms industry has been influenced by both military and civilian needs.

Soviet Era Development:

During the Soviet era, Russia became a major producer of small arms, including 30 bore pistols. The emphasis on simplicity, ruggedness, and ease of production resulted in models that were well-suited for military and law enforcement use.

Cold War Influence:

The Cold War era saw the widespread adoption of Russian-made 30 bore pistols by military and police forces in the Eastern Bloc and allied nations. Models like the Makarov PM gained international recognition for their durability and straightforward design.

Post-Soviet Era:

Following the dissolution of the Soviet Union, Russian firearm manufacturers continued to produce 30 bore pistols, catering to both domestic and export markets. Modern Russian-made pistols often incorporate updated features and materials while retaining the robustness of earlier designs.
